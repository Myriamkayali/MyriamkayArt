The artist

Myriam
Kayali

My work lives at the intersection of stillness and presence. Through figurative painting, I explore what it means to be human — the weight of a gaze, the curve of a shoulder, the quiet dignity of an unguarded moment.

Rooted in the landscapes and light of Lebanon, my palette draws from the warmth of the Mediterranean — ochres, terracottas, deep umbers — applied with a directness that I hope feels both intimate and enduring.

"I paint to remember that the ordinary is extraordinary — that a face, a gesture, a body at rest holds entire worlds."

I work primarily in oil on linen, with a process that values slowness and revision. Each painting undergoes weeks or months of sustained attention before it is complete.

My work has been collected by private collectors across Europe and the Middle East. I also accept commissions for portraits and figurative works — please reach out.
